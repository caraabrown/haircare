What's your hair's porosity?
Required
Porosity is the single most important factor in your protocol. It determines which products penetrate, how long moisture lasts, and whether protein helps or hurts you.
How to test your porosity →
Float test: Drop a clean, product-free strand in room temperature water. Wait 2-4 minutes.
• Floats near top = low porosity (cuticle is closed)
• Sinks slowly to middle = medium porosity
• Sinks to bottom fast = high porosity (cuticle is raised)
How would you describe your strand thickness?
Required
Fine strands are overwhelmed by heavy products and break under too much protein. This changes everything from DC timing to which oils you use.
How to tell →
Hold a single strand against a white piece of paper. Fine: barely visible. Medium: clearly visible. Coarse: looks thick, may feel wiry.
What's your scalp's density? (How much hair do you have?)

What's your home water type?
Medium
Hard water mineral buildup is one of the most common invisible causes of dryness and breakage — and most women never suspect it. Hard water deposits calcium and magnesium on every strand, blocking moisture from entering. This changes how often you need to clarify and whether a chelating shampoo belongs in your routine.
How to check your water type →
Search your zip code at whatsmyharwater.com, or check your local water utility's annual water quality report. Alternatively: if you have white mineral deposits on your faucets or showerhead, you have hard water.
